Thai style curry

This fragrant curry is inspired by Southern Thai cuisine. You'll make a punchy panang-style sauce with chicken,
peanut butter, coconut and red curry paste, then serve with rice and crispy onions.

Ingredients

  • 2 British chicken breasts
  • 20g Thai red curry paste
  • 15ml fish sauce
  • 26g smooth peanut butter
  • 130g basmati rice
  • 50g solid creamed coconut
  • 15g crispy onions
  • 2 dried lime leaves
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 red pepper
  • 1 brown onion

Cooking instructions

  1. Add the basmati rice and 300ml [600ml] cold water to a pot with a lid and bring to the boil over a high heat
    Once boiling, reduce the heat to very low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until all the water has been absorbed
    and the rice is cooked Once cooked, remove from the heat and keep covered until serving.

  2. Meanwhile, take your chicken out of the fridge and open the packet to let it breathe before cooking.
    Peel and finely slice the brown onion Deseed the red pepper and cut into thin strips.

  3. Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick) with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium heat.
    Once hot, add the sliced onion and pepper strips and cook for 4-5 min or until starting to soften.

  4. At this point, we recommend that you boil half a kettle of water. Then, chop the creamed coconut roughly (if required!).

  5. Dissolve the chopped creamed coconut and smooth peanut butter in 250ml boiled water.
    Add the ground turmeric, fish sauce, lime leaves and 1 tsp sugar this is your peanut stock.

  6. Cut the chicken breast portions into small bite-sized pieces.
    Once the pepper and onion has softened, add the chopped chicken and Thai red curry paste to the pan
    and cook for 3-4 min or until fragrant.

  7. Once fragrant, add the peanut stock and cook for 7-8 min further or until the chicken is cooked through
    (no pink meat!) and the sauce has thickened.
