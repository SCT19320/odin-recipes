Beef keema with Lime rice and Coriander

Description

Keema is an Indian street food made with mince and curry spices. For this sensational one-pot,
you'll pan fry the beef with rice and carrots, and lift the flavours with a squeeze of lime.

Ingredients

  • 15g fresh root ginger
  • 11g beef stock mix
  • 32g tomato paste
  • 130g basmati rice
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 10g coriander
  • 1 brown onion
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 lime
  • Extras: Salt, Pepper, sugar and Vegetable oil

Cooking instructions

  1. Peel and finely slice the brown onion, then Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick with a matching lid)
    with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a medium-high heat. Once hot, add the sliced onion with a pinch of salt and sugar
    and cook for 3 min, or until starting to soften, then add the beef mince and cook for a further 3 min.

  2. Meanwhile, top, tail, peel and chop the carrots into small, bite-sized pieces.
    Peel (scrape the skin off with a teaspoon) and grate (or finely chop) the ginger.
    Peel and grate (or finely chop) the garlic

  3. Add the chopped carrot, grated ginger and grated garlic to the pan and cook for 1 min.

  4. Add the beef stock mix, tomato paste and curry powder and give everything a good mix up.

  5. Stir the basmati rice into the pan.

  6. Add 300ml [600ml] cold water to the pan and bring to the boil over a high heat.
    Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cook, covered, for 12-15 min or until the water has been absorbed and the rice is cooked.
    Check and stir occasionally to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom.

  7. Meanwhile, cut the lime[s] in half and Chop the coriander finely, including the stalks (save some leaves for garnish!)
    Stir the chopped coriander and juice of 1/2 lime through the cooked rice.
    Serve the beef keema in bowls and garnish with a sprinkle of the reserved coriander leaves and a lime wedge to the side.

  8. This is your beef keema. Enjoy!
